🚨 Red Flags Checklist

⚠ 3 Red Flags / Yellow Flags Identified
●
High US Revenue Concentration (65-70% of Home Textile from US)
Trident derives ~65-70% of its home textile revenues (which is 52-53% of total revenue) from the US market — making it highly vulnerable to US tariff policy changes. The 26% reciprocal tariff announcement severely impacted Q3 FY26 performance.
●
Double CFO Exit in 6 Months (Jan 2026 + May 2026)
Group CFO Rahul Roongta resigned January 2026; CFO Avneesh Barua resigned May 29, 2026. Two senior finance exits in 6 months is unusual and warrants monitoring, though both cited personal reasons and the company holds IND AA credit rating.
●
ROCE Below Cost of Capital for 3 Consecutive Years
ROCE has been below 12% since FY23 (peaking at 23% in FY22), currently at 9.86%. This indicates the company is not generating returns above its cost of capital — a structural red flag unless margins recover.

3-Year Investment Thesis Catalysts

CATALYST 1 — UK FTA
India-UK FTA (2025) eliminates tariffs on Indian textile exports to UK. Full benefits materialise by FY27. UK could be a high-growth, margin-accretive diversification from US dependency.
CATALYST 2 — US TARIFF RESET
India at 18% tariff vs competition at 19-20%+. India-US trade deal (under negotiation) could further reduce tariffs, reversing the demand compression that caused Q3 FY26 collapse.
CATALYST 3 — MARGIN RECOVERY
EBITDA margin returning from 9% trough to 17-18% pre-FY23 levels would triple net profit on same revenue. At ₹700 Cr normalised PAT and 28× P/E, stock would be ~₹39. Cost optimisation programme underway.
